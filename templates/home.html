🧠 What is Stress?

Stress is the body's natural response to any demand or challenge — real or perceived. When you face pressure, your nervous system releases hormones like adrenaline and cortisol that prepare your body to respond. This is known as the "fight-or-flight" response.

While short-term stress can sharpen focus and boost performance, chronic occupational stress — stress that persists over weeks and months at work — can have serious consequences:

  • Physical: Headaches, fatigue, poor sleep, weakened immunity, high blood pressure
  • Emotional: Anxiety, irritability, depression, feeling overwhelmed or hopeless
  • Behavioural: Reduced productivity, social withdrawal, poor decision-making
  • Burnout: Complete physical and emotional exhaustion from prolonged stress
✅ Good news: Occupational stress is measurable, manageable, and treatable. The first step is understanding your stress levels — which is exactly what the OSI Assessment helps you do.
📊 What is the Occupational Stress Index (OSI)?

The Occupational Stress Index (OSI) is a scientifically validated psychometric tool developed by A.K. Srivastava & A.P. Singh (1981) at Banaras Hindu University. It is specifically designed to measure work-related stress among employed professionals, particularly in academic and organisational settings.

The OSI measures stress across 12 sub-scales, each targeting a distinct dimension of occupational stress. Scores range from -
46 to 230:

I. Role Overload
II. Role Ambiguity
III. Role Conflict
IV. Group & Political Pressure
V. Responsibility for Persons
VI. Under Participation
VII. Powerlessness
VIII. Poor Peer Relations
IX. Intrinsic Impoverishment
X. Low Status
XI. Strenuous Working Conditions
XII. Unprofitability
